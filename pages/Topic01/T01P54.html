Once IV access is established, start dextrose 10% or D10W IV fluid, without electrolytes, at a rate of 80 mL/kg/day.
This rate will provide a glucose infusion rate of 5.5 mg/kg/minute, which is similar to the liver glycogen breakdown and glucose production rate in healthy term newborns.
In the absence of conditions related to hyperinsulinemia, or inadequate glycogen stores, or increased glucose utilization, this glucose infusion rate is usually adequate to maintain the blood sugar within a normal range.
