Anticipate Birth Complications

  • Increased risk of shoulder dystocia, which occurs because of excessive fat deposition in the upper chest area
    • Injuries associated with shoulder dystocia include:
      • Perinatal
        asphyxia
      • Brachial plexus injury
      • Paralysis of the diaphragm
      • Injury to the recurrent laryngeal nerve
    • Other injuries include:
      • Fractures of the arm or clavicles
      • Injury to the liver
