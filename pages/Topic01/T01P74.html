Umbilical Vein Catheter (UVC) and Umbilical Artery Catheters (UAC)
Submit

Please review the additional information on Umbilical Vein Catheter (UVC) and Umbilical Artery Catheters (UAC).
A UVC should be selected for the following reasons:

  • If rapid IV access is required to deliver emergency medications and fluids
  • If there is ongoing difficulty establishing a peripheral IV in a reasonable time period or attempts
  • To administer glucose concentrations greater than 12.5% dextrose
  • To perform an exchange transfusion to treat severe hyperbilirubinemia

A UAC should be selected:

  • If continuous arterial blood pressure monitoring is required and/or arterial blood gas monitoring is needed
  • To assist with exchange transfusion. Remember that blood may be removed via the UVC or UAC but should only be infused via the UVC.

Please review the additional information on Umbilical Vein Catheter (UVC) and Umbilical Artery Catheters (UAC).

  • All medications, including vasopressors, can be given through the UVC once the UVC is inserted and the catheter tip is in good position.
  • If a peripheral IV is not available, then blood and blood products may be given through the UVC. However, some institutions do not permit platelet transfusion through a UVC, so check your hospital policy regarding platelet transfusions.
  • Hyperalimentation fluid may be given via the UVC, but it is not recommended for administration through the UAC.
  • The UAC should not be used for medication or blood administration.
