Note: Some countries outside of the United States consider late preterm gestation to be between 35-0/7 and 36-6/7 weeks.

Infants born before 37 weeks are metabolically and physiologically immature. The earlier the gestation, the more pronounced this becomes.

In addition to being at higher risk for hypoglycemia, late preterm infants are at increased risk for:

  • Poor feeding ability
  • Temperature instability
  • Hypothermia
  • Respiratory distress
  • Hyperbilirubinemia
  • Increased hospital readmission rates
  • 3-fold higher mortality rate than term infants
