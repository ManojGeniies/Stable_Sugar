Other definitions of SGA include when a birth weight is more than 2 standard deviations below the mean for gestational age, or when the birth weight is below the 3rd or 5th percentile.

Growth in utero is influenced by a variety of factors including genetics, the ability of the placenta to adequately deliver oxygen and nutrients to the fetus, and intrauterine growth factors and hormones. When compared with appropriately grown, AGA infants, SGA infants have higher complication rates, including death.
