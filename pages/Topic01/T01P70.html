Use your good judgment to if the blood sugar is 30 or less and you give a glucose bolus, check the blood sugar 30 minutes after the bolus is completed.

At times, clinicians want to verify the accuracy of a low point-of-care bedside glucose test by sending a sample of blood to the lab for plasma glucose testing. However, do not delay treatment while awaiting confirmatory lab test results. Continue monitoring the blood glucose until a pattern of stability has been achieved and the glucose remains consistently in the recommended normal range of 50 to 110 mg/dL (2.8 - 6.1 mmol/L).
