In utero, the fetus relies almost exclusively on the placental transfer of glucose and nutrients to meet growth and energy demands.
Fetal glucose values change throughout gestation for a variety of reasons, but in general, glucose value is approximately 70% to 80% of the maternal value.
If the mother's glucose is 100 mg/dL, or 5.6 mmol/L, what would the approximate fetal glucose value be? (Select an option from the drop-down menu)
That is correct.
The fetus regulates blood glucose levels by secreting insulin. This will become an important point when we discuss maternal diabetes and the effect of maternal hyperglycemia on fetal glucose values and insulin production and secretion.
