• When infants are sick, it is safest to withhold feedings and establish IV access.
  • The recommended IV fluid and rate are D10W, without electrolytes, at 80 mL/kg/day. If the infant is older than 24 hours and renal function is normal, then consider adding electrolytes to the IV fluid.
  • Monitor the blood sugar at an appropriate interval relevant to the infant’s risk factors, and maintain the blood sugar between 50 and 110 mg/dL (2.8 and 6.1 mmol/L).
  • If the blood sugar is less than 50 mg/dL (2.8 mmol/L), give a 2 mL/kg bolus of D10W and continue the IV infusion of D10W at 80 mL/kg/day.
  • Evaluate the blood sugar soon after a bolus is completed, and use good clinical judgment to determine how often the blood sugar should be evaluated once a pattern of stability has been established. 
  • The highest concentration of dextrose that can be safely infused by peripheral IV is D12.5W. 
  • If greater than D12.5W dextrose concentration is required, the fluid should be infused through a central venous line. 
  • And finally, if the blood sugar is persistently low, then consult neonatal experts for guidance as needed.
