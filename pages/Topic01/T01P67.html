For sick infants who are not well enough to be enterally fed, The S.T.A.B.L.E. Program recommends the following:

Step 1: Evaluate the blood sugar within 1 to 2 hours after birth or at the time the infant becomes unwell.

Step 2: Continue monitoring every 1 to 3 hours based on the glucose values, the interventions provided, and the health status of the infant—that is, stability of vital signs and whether youâ€™re able to feed the infant or whether the infant requires IV therapy.

Step 3: Increase the monitoring interval based on the infant's risk factors and response to therapy and maintain the blood sugar between 50 and 110 mg/dL (2.8–6.1 mmol/L).
