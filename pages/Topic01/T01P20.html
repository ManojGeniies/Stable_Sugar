A 24-gauge IV catheter is the standard size used with neonatal patients.
If a 24-gauge IV catheter is not available, a 23-or 25-gauge butterfly needle may be used.
A transilluminator or bright penlight may help make the veins more visible. Make sure the light does not heat up when turned on.

Starting IVs and how to secure IVs

Once an IV is inserted, inspect the IV site at least hourly.
Record what is infusing as well as the amount infused.
Examine the IV site frequently in case the IV dislodges or infiltrates.
