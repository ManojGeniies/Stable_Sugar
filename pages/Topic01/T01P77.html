Heparin is commonly added to central line fluids to prevent clots from forming at the end of the catheter or in the blood vessel where the catheter is located.
 
  • Heparin is supplied in different concentrations.
  • Double-check that the correct concentration has been selected each time a vial is
    opened.
  • Ideally, commercially prepared IV fluid containing heparin should be utilized.
 
  • If this is not possible, it is advised that two nurses double-check the heparin dose and the concentration of heparin in the vial.
  • It only takes 50 to 100 units of heparin per kilogram of body weight to “heparinize” an infant.
  • This can lead to excessive and sometimes devastating bleeding.
