Medications

Which umbilical catheter should be used for the following items? Select the appropriate option from the drop-down menu.

  • For giving vasopressors
  • For blood and blood product administration
  • For exchange transfusion
  • For hyperalimentation fluid administration

*Vasopressors include Dopamine, Dobutamine, Epinephrine, Norepinephrine, Vasopressin, Phenylephrine

Submit

That is correct.

  • All medications, including vasopressors can be given through the UVC once the UVC is inserted and the catheter tip is in good position.
  • If a peripheral IV is not available, then blood and blood products may also be given through the UVC.
  • If an exchange transfusion is required, then it is common to use the UVC for the procedure.
  • The UAC may be used for withdrawal of the infant's blood with concurrent administration of the replacement blood through the UVC.
  • Hyperalimentation fluid may be given via the UVC but it is not recommended for administration through the UAC.
  • The UAC should not be used for medication or blood administration.
