If malrotation or bowel obstruction is suspected, keep the following in mind:
 
If the baby has bilious (green-colored) emesis, an upper gastrointestinal contrast study is an appropriate diagnostic test.
If malrotation with midgut volvulus is diagnosed, emergency surgery is indicated to untwist the bowel.
If suction equipment is not available, gently aspirate with a syringe on the gastric tube every 10 minutes.
When possible, diagnostic studies should be done at a tertiary hospital where surgical services are available.
If bowel obstruction is suspected, withhold feedings and insert an IV and then provide D10W IV fluid.
