The gold standard for measuring the level of glucose in the bloodstream is the plasma glucose value.
This measurement requires a sample of whole blood to be obtained and processed by the laboratory.
 
Its accuracy is dependent upon timely processing by the laboratory.
Glucose levels may decline as much as 15 to 20 mg/dL, or 0.8 to 1.1 mmol/L, per hour when the blood sample stands at room temperature.
